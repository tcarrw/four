Kendyl was the gate. Not a fate-thread to follow, but a mirror — angled just right to show you a version of yourself you had stopped acknowledging. The significance wasn’t in her staying. It was in what she reflected: the part of you that was ready to be seen again, the clarity you thought you had misplaced, the desire to move forward without permission. Mirrors don’t accompany you; they illuminate you. Kendyl did not arrive to become a chapter — she arrived to reveal the threshold. You walked through it. That was always the real story.
