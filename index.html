This page is a listening room. Patterns are suggestions, not conclusions.
quiet guide — what to listen for

pronouns: notice how “I” and “you” move — are they facing each other or describing from afar?

melody vs camera: when does the line feel like speech, and when does it feel like a lens observing?

refrain: if a hook repeats, does its meaning change or stay fixed?

color words: gold, grey, blue, white — what kind of truth do they signal?

tempo / meter: waltz time (3/4, 6/8) often feels like memory — who owns it in the scene?

don’t decide too soon. patterns develop like film — quietly, in the dark.

for readers — how to view Willow Bowery legally (free options)

Amazon preview: use “Look Inside / Read sample” on the product page to view a handful of pages (official and safe). Example: Tortured Poets — Amazon.

Kindle Unlimited: if you already have KU (or a promo trial), some self-published titles can be read in full at no extra cost. Check the book page for KU eligibility.

Library borrowing: many public libraries lend eBooks via Libby/OverDrive; when available, choose “Read with Kindle.” Start here: Libby app.

Note: avoid full-PDF sites; they’re often unauthorized and unsafe. None of this is required — the listening room stands on its own.
